# Volume 2: Implementation, Governance, and Clinical Translation

_Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare, Volume 2_

> **Status: Coming soon.** Volume 2 is in final production. The notebooks below are available now; the editable governance templates from the appendices will be added here on release.

This directory holds the **5 hands-on notebooks** for Volume 2. Where Volume 1 is notebook-heavy, Volume 2 pairs a focused set of notebooks with a library of governance templates and checklists drawn from the appendices.

**Central theme:** regulatory clearance is necessary but not sufficient. FDA clearance or CE marking shows that a device met performance thresholds on validation data at one point in time. It does not guarantee performance at your organization, with your patients, in your workflow, or over time.

## Naming convention

Files follow `notebook_NL_title.ipynb`, where `N` is the chapter number and `L` is a letter sequence within the chapter, matching the in-book labels (1A, 2A, 2B, 5A, 6A).

## Chapters

Ten chapters across five parts:

- **Part I: Validation and Security.** Clinical Validation and Evidence; Security, Privacy, and Robustness.
- **Part II: Regulatory and Procurement.** Regulatory Pathways (FDA and EU); Procurement and Vendor Management.
- **Part III: Implementation.** Deployment and Integration; AI Maturity and Success Metrics.
- **Part IV: Evaluation and Synthesis.** The Seven Journeys (Implementation Analysis); Critical Reading of AI Research; Evaluating Vendors and Technology Claims.
- **Part V: Looking Forward.** Advanced and Emerging Topics.

## About this volume

Volume 2 moves from how AI works to how it reaches patients safely and effectively, covering clinical validation, security and robustness, regulatory pathways, procurement, deployment and EHR integration, maturity and governance, critical reading of AI evidence, and the evaluation of vendor claims.
